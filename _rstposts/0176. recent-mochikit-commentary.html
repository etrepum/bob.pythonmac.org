--- categories: javascript, MochiKit, AJAX date: 2005/09/08 11:34:04 guid: http://bob.pythonmac.org/?p=176 permalink: http://bob.pythonmac.org/archives/2005/09/08/recent-mochikit-commentary/ tags: '' title: Recent MochiKit commentary --- .. -*- mode: rst -*- For those not watching the entire blogosphere, here's two pretty good articles/posts about `MochiKit`_ that have come up recently: .. _`MochiKit`: http://mochikit.com/ ---- `Andy`_ has put together `part 1`_ of a series of MochiKit tutorials for building a blog application, almost entirely client-side: http://argv0.net/static/mochitut/1/ .. _`part 1`: http://argv0.net/static/mochitut/1/ .. _`Andy`: http://argv0.net/ For a little more commentary on that post, you might look towards the `Ajaxian blog coverage`_. .. _`Ajaxian blog coverage`: http://www.ajaxian.com/archives/2005/09/simple_ajax_wit.html ---- `Jeff Shell`_ put out a little head-to-head between `MochiKit and Prototype`_ from a Zope user's perspective: http://griddlenoise.blogspot.com/2005/09/prototypejs-and-mochikit.html .. _`MochiKit and Prototype`: http://griddlenoise.blogspot.com/2005/09/prototypejs-and-mochikit.html .. _`Jeff Shell`: http://griddlenoise.blogspot.com/ What Jeff doesn't seem to mention in his article is that his AJAX example actually shows Prototype and MochiKit living in harmony (using ``Element.show`` and ``Element.fade`` from Prototype, with MochiKit's AJAX support). That actually *does* work. Personally, I'd have demonstrated the AJAX stuff without the Fully.Qualified.Names in that scenario (to show MochiKit in its best light in comparison w/ Prototype), and used ``showElement`` instead of ``Element.show`` (saves a character, removes a Prototype dependency ;). MochiKit plays very nice with both the strictest (i.e. `Dojo`_) and the loosest JavaScript development styles (i.e. Prototype) around, because it's very defensively programmed and well exercised in those scenarios. There's one or two places in MochiKit where you pay a price (performance, or extraneous data carried around) if you mucked up ``Object.prototype``, but it actually does let you do that. .. _`Dojo`: http://dojotoolkit.org/ ---- Thanks guys!
